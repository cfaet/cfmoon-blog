I recently started a remote gig and the company asked me to set up
HubStaff or my hours would not count.
So what is Hubstaff? It's essentially a monitoring and time-tracking tool that +spies+
tracks your actions to see if you're working as intended. They claim
not to perform keystroke logging, access the camera or monitor your
email, but since the software is proprietary, more digging is required.

** Is it safe?
Personally, I'm still evaluating its safety and whether it really
respects some of your privacy at least, but someone with more time and
expertise should do this, and there's probably articles about its
safety as Hubstaff (both the company and the software) are not really
recent, it was established in 2012 according to Wikipedia.
* Under this pressure, under this weight
Indeed, I installed. But my OS was not a common one, and at first
thought I expected it to be Windows only, but surprisingly, they have
builds for Mac and Linux. From what I could gather, Hubstaff is
written in C++, making it portable indeed. So which system do I use?
NixOS, a very niche but highly technical OS for fans of reproducible
builds and functional programming nerds (like me), this actually gives
NixOS an edge as its used by very capable people, and often very
knowledgeable, experienced engineers and specialists.
** Is it available?
Yes, it's available on NixOS, and you don't need to do any manual building,
porting or patching, as the NixOS contributors already packaged it,

~Be aware that you need the unstable NixOS version, 26.05~

- You can either run it via nix-shell or nix shell (flake-based)
#+begin_src sh
  nix-shell -p hubstaff
  HubstaffClient # Yes, this is the actual exe name
#+end_src
#+begin_src sh
  nix shell nixpkgs#hubstaff
  HubstaffClient # Yes, this is the actual exe name
#+end_src

You can also install it by adding it to your package list, via
home-manager or otherwise.

~Be aware, you'll need to enable unfree software to install Hubstaff~

#+begin_src nix
  {pkgs, ...}:
  nixpkgs.config.allowUnfree
  enviroment.systemPackages = with pkgs; [
    hubstaff
  ]
#+end_src
